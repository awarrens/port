# Mood: Field Notes

## Essence
An editorial portfolio that feels handmade and well-travelled — like a printed zine flipped open on a wooden desk, sunlight warming one corner of the page. Crafted, soulful, and quietly confident: the work speaks first, the typography pays attention, and there's room around everything to breathe. Warm earth tones meet clean grotesk type and the occasional collaged moment, signalling a designer who reads, photographs, and thinks before they ship.

## Colour
- **Temperature:** Warm-leaning neutral. Sun-on-clay warmth offset by cool forest and deep black for grounding.
- **Palette:**
  - `#f3efe6` "paper cream" — primary background
  - `#1a1a1a` "ink black" — primary text, hero canvases
  - `#c8541e` "terracotta" — accent / hover / highlight
  - `#8b2e1f` "burnt sienna" — secondary accent, tiles
  - `#d99a3a` "ochre" — supporting warm
  - `#1f4a36` "forest" — grounding green
  - `#a3c46a` "moss lime" — lift / unexpected pop
  - `#6b6b66` "weathered stone" — meta text, labels
- **Contrast:** High where it matters (display headlines on cream or black), low everywhere else. Body copy sits in muted, comfortable contrast — not punchy.
- **Saturation:** Muted with one or two saturated accents per view. Earth pigment, not screen-bright. Think dyed wool, not neon.

## Typography
- **Character:** A duet — an expressive serif with a little personality (italic ligatures, almost editorial) paired with a precise modern grotesk for everything functional. Small ALL-CAPS labels in monospace or tracked grotesk for nav, metadata, captions.
- **Weight distribution:** Big jumps. Display weight is heavy or italic-display; supporting copy is regular; meta is small, tracked, and quiet. Almost nothing lives in the middle.
- **Density:** Airy. Generous leading, room around headlines, captions floating with breathing space.
- **Suggested pairings:**
  - Display serif: *PP Editorial New*, *GT Sectra*, *Tiempos Headline*, or *Canela*
  - Body grotesk: *Inter*, *Söhne*, *Neue Haas Grotesk*, or *GT America*
  - Meta / labels: *JetBrains Mono*, *IBM Plex Mono*, or tracked grotesk small caps

## Space & Layout
- **Rhythm:** Generous, asymmetric, editorial. Wide margins. Content blocks anchored to a grid but allowed to break it occasionally — a portrait pushed off-axis, a caption stranded in whitespace.
- **Grid character:** Fluid with a strong underlying structure. Mosaic-friendly: squircle/rounded-square tiles for image collections, but also classic two-column editorial spreads for prose.
- **Scale relationships:** Dramatic. Hero typography is large enough to feel printed; body text stays modest. Images range from postage-stamp thumbnails (deliberate restraint) to full-bleed (deliberate impact). The contrast in scale *is* the hierarchy.

## Texture & Material
- **Surface quality:** Mostly flat, but with hints of paper, dye, and analog texture — soft grain on photography, subtle warm wash behind hero sections, occasional collage cut-outs.
- **Material references:** Uncoated paper stock, postage stamps, dyed linen, dried grass, ceramic glaze, kraft envelope.
- **Finish:** Matte. Nothing glossy, nothing glassy. If light catches anything, it's the sheen of a printed page, not a screen.

## Emotional Register
Walking into a small independent bookshop on a Saturday afternoon — the smell of paper, low music, someone's coffee cooling on the counter. The viewer should feel they've encountered a *person*, not a template: curious, well-read, attentive to craft, unhurried. Cultural touchpoints: *Kinfolk* and *Apartamento* magazines, Nike's Space Hippie editorial system, Aesop's storefront restraint, a 1970s Japanese stamp collection, a really good Letterboxd profile. Confident without shouting.

## Design Principles
- **The work is the hero** — chrome, navigation, and decoration recede; photography and writing carry the page.
- **Warmth over polish** — choose paper-cream and terracotta over white-and-grey; choose hand-set asymmetry over perfect alignment.
- **Big or quiet, never medium** — type and image either lead the page or step fully out of the way; nothing competes at half-volume.
- **One unexpected note per view** — a moss-lime accent, an italic display word, a single off-grid element. Surprise sparingly.
- **Editorial, not corporate** — page structures borrow from magazines and zines, not SaaS landing pages.

## References
- **Mosaic of squircle tiles (orange/green/red, "Explore the Children…")** — Establishes the rounded-tile mosaic pattern, warm-saturated palette of clay/forest/burnt-red on charcoal, and the small-caps meta labels under each tile. Use for thumbnail grids and project indexes.
- **"antezîa / textures" collage** — Confirms the editorial-collage instinct: orange and pink colour blocks, a halftone object, an oversized italic serif, a green ornamental motif, all on near-black. Signal: typography can play with photography, not just sit beside it.
- **"SLEEP · LOFI BEATS · CALMING PIANO…" type-with-images** — Photographic glyphs nested inside display type. Shows that imagery can live *inside* letters, not only beside them. A possible treatment for a hero headline.
- **Nike Space Hippie editorial slide** — System-led editorial: large grotesk with inline thumbnails, underscored meta numbering (`_01`, `_02`), green accent on near-black. Influences how meta/numbering and inline imagery can read together.
- **StandardFleet landing page** — Cream paper background, small tracked grotesk nav, generous whitespace, lime-yellow accent block, big serif headlines. The closest single-image reference for the actual landing structure.
- **Archimedes Blocks brand** — Torn/sliced collage of a single photograph against bold green geometric blocks, with bilingual type. Permission to be playful with photo cropping and to layer geometric colour shapes.
- **"Nature is Celestial" stamps composition** — Tiny editorial details: postage-stamp framing, micro-typography, deep moody black, ornate pattern. Reference for footer / micro-detail treatments.
- **"Porter / nurture / Get your wish" rounded-tile composite** — Confirms the squircle/rounded-rectangle module on black with photographic content and label chips in corners. A possible card style.
- **Simantic studio site** — Editorial portfolio archetype: thin tracked grotesk nav across the top, a horizontal row of expressive photographs, oceans of whitespace, restrained chrome. Strongest layout reference for the landing itself.
- **Millls portfolio** — Two-line manifesto headlines as section dividers, mixed-aspect image strips, micro-captions. Use for the about/skills section structure.
- **Clara Misa portfolio** — Clean cream canvas, tidy project rows with paragraph + image grid, tiny meta links top-right ("About, Contact, Instagram, Are.na"). Direct precedent for the landing's About/Contact organisation.

## Anti-References
- **Corporate SaaS dashboards** — no gradient hero blobs, no purple-to-blue, no oversized rounded buttons with drop shadows.
- **Glassmorphism, neumorphism, heavy 3D** — no frosted panels, no soft inner shadows, no glossy spheres.
- **Generic "minimal" tech portfolio** — pure white background, single sans serif, dark-mode toggle as a personality; the absence of taste is not taste.
- **Bright-startup palettes** — Stripe purple, Linear iridescence, default Tailwind sky-500. Saturation must feel pigmented, not pixel-pure.
- **Over-animated scroll spectacle** — no parallax-on-everything, no scroll-jacked story chapters, no cursor trails. Motion is a seasoning, not a meal.

## Agent Instructions
You are designing a single-page personal portfolio landing (about, photos, contact, skills) for someone whose taste is editorial, warm, and crafted. Set the page on `#f3efe6` (paper cream), body type in a clean grotesk like Inter or Söhne at ~16px with comfortable line-height (~1.6), and reserve a high-contrast display serif (PP Editorial New or GT Sectra) for the one or two largest headlines only. Use small, letter-spaced ALL-CAPS grotesk or monospace for navigation, section labels, and metadata. Lean on whitespace — wide margins, generous vertical rhythm, no card-on-card density. Photographs should breathe: prefer a horizontal row of medium-sized photos with air around them (Simantic-style) over a tight grid. When you need a colour accent, reach for terracotta `#c8541e`, ochre `#d99a3a`, forest `#1f4a36`, or — sparingly — moss lime `#a3c46a`; never use pure black on pure white, never use SaaS purples or sky-blues, never reach for gradients or glass. Allow one editorial flourish per page (an italic display word, an asymmetric photo, a single coloured rule) but keep the rest restrained. Hover and motion should be quiet: subtle colour shifts, a slow fade, a small translate — no bouncing, no parallax, no scroll-jacking. The finished page should feel like a well-printed magazine spread that happens to be a website, not a SaaS landing page that happens to have a photo.
